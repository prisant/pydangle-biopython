Michael G. Prisant, B.S. (Yale, Chemistry and English), M.A. (Columbia, Chemistry),
Ph.D. (Stanford, with Dick Zare — electrochemistry, reaction dynamics, laser spectroscopy).

Chemical physicist. Former Professor of Chemistry and Computer Science at Duke University.
Retired July 2025 from position as Senior Research Scientist in the Richardson Laboratory,
Duke Biochemistry.

**Deep expertise:** Experimental and theoretical reaction dynamics; laser spectroscopy;
theory of intermolecular forces; computational geometry; protein validation.

**Post-doctoral and faculty-level positions:** John Polanyi (Toronto, Nobel Laureate) —
reaction dynamics/laser spectroscopy; Charles Harris (Berkeley) — electrochemistry/surface
science/ultrafast laser spectroscopy; Raphael Levine (Fritz Haber Institute, Jerusalem) —
theoretical reaction dynamics; Bill Jackson (UC Davis) — comet spectroscopy; Sydney Leach
& Guy Taieb (CNRS Orsay) — photophysics; Michael Menzinger (Toronto) — molecular beam
chemiluminescence; Jim Wright (Carleton) — ab initio quantum mechanics of HFH.
Faculty-level positions at Toronto, CNRS, Paris-Sud, CEA Saclay, Grenoble, Hebrew
University, Duke, NC State, UNC.

**Duke faculty research:** Experimental surface science (halogen adsorption/reaction on
rare gas surfaces); cinematic visualization of surface reactions and biomolecules;
ray-representations for volumetric properties of protein space-filling models.

**Duke senior scientist:** Protein design and parallel supercomputer
construction/deployment/administration for Homme Hellinga; protein validation studies
and systems administration for Dave and Jane Richardson (MolProbity).

**Current work:** Collaborating with Ezra Miller's group (Duke Math) on Topological
Data Analysis (TDA) applied to protein Ramachandran maps. Pydangle is the base tool
for this work. Knows Fred Cazals at INRIA.

**Working style:** Methodical — reviews changes before committing, prefers incremental
development with documentation kept in sync, wants to understand trade-offs before
choosing approaches.
